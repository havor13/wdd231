Discover Kumasi – Chamber of Commerce

History of Kumasi

Founding Era (1695)

Kumasi was established as the capital of the Ashanti Kingdom by Osei Tutu I and Komfo Anokye. The name comes from the Twi phrase meaning “under the Kum tree.”

Expansion & Conflicts (18th–19th Century)

Kumasi grew into a powerful city-state, endured Anglo–Ashanti Wars, and symbolized unity through the Golden Stool. The city was burned by the British in 1874 but rebuilt by the Ashanti.

Colonial Rule (1896–1957)

Captured by the British in 1896, Kumasi was rebuilt with colonial infrastructure. Despite suppression, the Manhyia Palace remained the symbolic seat of the Asantehene.

Modern Growth (1957–Present)

After Ghana’s independence, Kumasi flourished as Ghana’s cultural capital, blending tradition with modern commerce, and today is home to over 3 million residents.

Demographics

Kumasi is a diverse city with over 3 million residents, blending tradition and modern commerce.
