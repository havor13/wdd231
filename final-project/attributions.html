Data Sources

The resource listings featured in the Ghanaian E‑Learning Hub are based on a curated dataset of institutions and initiatives across Ghana. These include universities, mentorship programs, bootcamps, and community hubs. The following organizations are acknowledged:

  • Kumasi Tech Circle – Local meetups and workshops on accessible web development and digital skills.
  • Ashesi University Online – Online courses in leadership, computer science, and entrepreneurship.
  • MEST Africa Academy – Mentorship and training for aspiring software entrepreneurs.
  • KNUST E‑Learning – Distance learning programs in engineering, business, and social sciences.
  • Ghana Coding Bootcamp – Intensive bootcamp covering HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Python.
  • Accra Digital Centre – Innovation hub offering coworking, training, and digital literacy programs.
  • Ghana Open University – Open access courses in arts, sciences, and technology.
  • Women in Tech Ghana – Community supporting women in technology through events and mentorship.
  • Ghana Accessibility Network – Network promoting inclusive design and accessibility standards.
  • African Leadership University Online – Pan‑African online programs in leadership and innovation.
  • Ghana STEM Mentors – Mentorship program connecting students with STEM professionals.
  • E‑Learning Africa Conference – Annual conference showcasing innovations in e‑learning across Africa.
  • Ghana MOOCs Hub – Aggregated MOOCs from global providers tailored for Ghanaian learners.
  • Tech Needs Girls – Mentorship initiative encouraging girls to pursue coding and STEM careers.
  • Ghana Digital Skills Portal – Government‑backed portal offering free digital skills training.

This dataset was compiled for educational purposes to highlight the diversity of e‑learning opportunities available to Ghanaian learners.
